# **Content Database: Officer Trauma Traits**

## **Mechanics Overview**

In the Sector Map Campaign, if a Bridge Officer ends a combat or event node with their Stress meter completely full, their mind snaps under the pressure of the Hegemony's pursuit.  
During the **At-Ease Phase** (Post-Combat), their Stress is reset to 0, but they permanently gain one **Trauma Trait** rolled from this database.

* Traumas persist across all future missions.  
* An Officer can have multiple Traumas.  
* Traumas can only be cured by spending **20 RP** at a Medical Station during a Drydock \[Haven\] node.  
* *Note: If an Officer gains a Trauma, you may roll 1D20, or the Fleet Admiral may choose the one that makes the most narrative sense based on how the mission went wrong.*

## **1\. General Traumas (Applies to Any Station)**

**1\. Shell-Shocked**

* **Flavor:** *Every shudder of the bulkheads brings them back to the Kaelen ambush.*  
* **Effect:** Whenever your ship takes Hull damage from an enemy attack, this officer immediately gains \+1 Stress.

**2\. Defeatist**

* **Flavor:** *They have done the math. They know the Hegemony is going to win.*  
* **Effect:** If the fleet's current Fleet Favor (FF) is below 0, this officer starts every combat scenario with 2 Stress already on their meter.

**3\. Insubordinate**

* **Flavor:** *They no longer trust High Command's judgment, only their own.*  
* **Effect:** If the War Council chooses *not* to override the Admiral's Rule of Engagement (RoE) at the start of a mission, this officer gains \+1 Stress every round during Phase 1\.

**4\. Pessimist**

* **Flavor:** *Their threshold for panic has been permanently lowered.*  
* **Effect:** This officer's Maximum Stress Limit is permanently reduced by 1\.

**5\. Flincher**

* **Flavor:** *Proximity alarms trigger an uncontrollable fight-or-flight response.*  
* **Effect:** Whenever an Enemy Capital Ship ends its movement in a hex adjacent to your ship, this officer gains \+1 Stress.

**6\. Lethargic**

* **Flavor:** *The stim-addiction is catching up to them. They can barely keep their eyes open.*  
* **Effect:** If this officer repeats the same action twice in one round, they suffer a \+2 Fatigue Penalty to their Stress instead of the standard \+1.

**7\. Micromanager**

* **Flavor:** *They refuse to delegate, demanding complex authorizations for basic tasks.*  
* **Effect:** The very first time a Command Token (CT) is assigned to this officer's station each round, it costs an additional \+1 CT from the Captain's pool to execute.

## **2\. Station-Specific Traumas**

### **A. Helm (Navigation & Evasion)**

**8\. Claustrophobic**

* **Flavor:** *They hyperventilate whenever the ship gets too close to the rocks.*  
* **Effect:** If the ship ends Phase 3 (Execution) in a hex adjacent to an Asteroid Field or Debris Field, this officer gains \+2 Stress.

**9\. Tunnel Vision**

* **Flavor:** *They lock their eyes forward and refuse to check the lateral drift monitors.*  
* **Effect:** The "Rotate" action now costs 2 Stress to perform instead of 1\.

**10\. Over-Cautious**

* **Flavor:** *They refuse to push the engines past factory safety limits.*  
* **Effect:** While this officer is operating the Helm, your ship's Maximum Speed is permanently capped at 2\.

### **B. Tactical (Weapons)**

**11\. Gun-Shy**

* **Flavor:** *Their hands shake too much to calibrate the targeting reticles properly.*  
* **Effect:** This officer's Tactical Skill Die is permanently stepped down by one tier (e.g., a Veteran D6 becomes a Rookie D4).

**12\. Tremors**

* **Flavor:** *They are terrified of handling live warheads after a near-miss in the magazine.*  
* **Effect:** This officer can no longer execute the "Load Ordnance" action. Weapons with the \[Ordnance\] tag are useless while they are on shift.

**13\. Trigger Happy**

* **Flavor:** *Silence is worse than the gunfire. If they aren't shooting, they're panicking.*  
* **Effect:** If this officer does not execute a "Fire Primary" action during a combat round, they automatically gain \+2 Stress during Phase 4\.

### **C. Engineering (Damage Control & Shields)**

**14\. Resource Hoarder**

* **Flavor:** *They are convinced the supply lines are dead and refuse to use spare parts.*  
* **Effect:** The "Damage Control" action (repairing Hull) now costs 3 CT to execute instead of 2\.

**15\. Over-Compensator**

* **Flavor:** *They obsessively route power to the front deflectors, ignoring the rest of the ship.*  
* **Effect:** When using the "Reinforce Shields" action, this officer *must* target the Fore Sector unless the Fore Sector is already at its maximum value.

**16\. Reckless Abandon**

* **Flavor:** *They bypass all safety regulators to get results, no matter the cost.*  
* **Effect:** Whenever this officer uses the "Reroute Power" action to generate bonus CT, the ship takes 1 unblockable Hull damage from electrical fires.

### **D. Sensors (EW & Targeting)**

**17\. Analysis Paralysis**

* **Flavor:** *They are drowning in data, unable to isolate the relevant tactical telemetry.*  
* **Effect:** The "Target Lock" action no longer provides a \-2 TN modifier. Instead, it only allows the attacker to reroll exactly one failed die in their Volley Pool.

**18\. Hyper-Vigilant**

* **Flavor:** *They are constantly running Level 5 diagnostic sweeps, draining the grid.*  
* **Effect:** Every action on the Sensors station costs an additional \+1 Stress to perform.

**19\. Comms-Phobic**

* **Flavor:** *They are convinced the Hegemony is listening and refuse to open tight-beam channels.*  
* **Effect:** This officer cannot use the "Cyber-Warfare" action, and your ship cannot be the target of Allied support actions (like CIC Sync or Repair Drones).

**20\. Phantom Scanners**

* **Flavor:** *They jump at shadows, convinced the sensors are picking up cloaked ships.*  
* **Effect:** At the start of Phase 1, roll a D6. On a 1 or 2, this officer gains \+1 Stress immediately from a false alarm.
