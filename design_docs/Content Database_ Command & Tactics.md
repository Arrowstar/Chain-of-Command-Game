# **Content Database: Command & Tactics**

## **1\. The Rules of Engagement (RoE) Mechanic**

During Game Setup, after the Scenario and Enemy Forces have been generated, the War Council must draw one **Rule of Engagement (RoE)** card from the Admiral's Deck.  
This card represents High Command's overarching operational parameter for the mission. It alters the fundamental rules of the game for the entire scenario.

### **The Override Mechanic (Insubordination)**

The RoE is mandatory. However, commanders on the ground sometimes have to disobey orders to keep their crew alive.

* At the start of Phase 1 (The Briefing Phase) of *any* round, the War Council may unanimously vote to **Override** the Admiral's RoE.  
* **The Cost:** Overriding an RoE immediately costs **\-3 Fleet Favor (FF)**.  
* **The Effect:** The RoE card is discarded and its rules no longer apply for the rest of the mission. High Command is furious, but you are free to fight on your own terms.

### **The Payout**

If the fleet manages to complete the scenario without Overriding the RoE, they receive a **\+2 FF Bonus** during the post-game wrap-up for exemplary obedience.

## **2\. The Admiral's Deck: Rules of Engagement**

The deck is divided into thematic doctrines, reflecting the different (and often callous) priorities of Hegemony High Command.

### **Doctrine: Maximum Aggression**

*High Command demands overwhelming force and refuses to acknowledge the concept of retreat.*

* **Zero-Tolerance for Cowardice:** \* *The Rule:* The Hegemony does not retreat. If any Player Ship ends Phase 3 (Execution) at a Current Speed of 0, OR further away from the nearest Enemy Ship than it started the round, the Helm Officer immediately suffers 2 Stress.  
* **Ramming Speed Authorized:**  
  * *The Rule:* Ammunition is secondary to momentum. All collisions (ramming) now deal **2D6 unblockable Hull damage** to both parties instead of 1D4. *Tactical Note: If you brought heavy armor, use your ship as a weapon.*  
* **Collateral Damage Approved:**  
  * *The Rule:* The target is the only thing that matters. All weapons with Area of Effect (Flak) or line-piercing capabilities deal \+1 Damage. However, if a player's attack misses its intended target completely, it automatically strikes the nearest Allied unit in its line of sight.

### **Doctrine: Resource Starvation**

*The fleet's supply lines are stretched thin. The Admiral expects you to win with scraps.*

* **Ammunition Conservation Protocol:**  
  * *The Rule:* Every shot must count. Whenever a Player Ship rolls an attack Volley Pool that results in zero hits, the Tactical Officer immediately suffers 2 Stress from the sheer panic of wasting ordnance.  
* **Power Grid Rationing:**  
  * *The Rule:* High Command is throttling your reactor outputs. Reduce the Base CT Generation of all Player Ships by 1\.  
* **"Hold Together With Duct Tape":**  
  * *The Rule:* Supply depots are empty; save the spare parts. The Engineering "Damage Control" action (Hull Repair) now costs 3 CT instead of 2\.

### **Doctrine: Cruel Calculus**

*The Admiral views the crew as highly expendable data points.*

* **Acceptable Losses:**  
  * *The Rule:* The Admiral expects casualties today. If a Player Capital Ship is destroyed (reduced to 0 Hull), the fleet does *not* suffer the standard \-3 FF penalty. Instead, gain **\+1 FF** for providing valuable combat data through your sacrifice.  
* **Live-Fire Telemetry:**  
  * *The Rule:* High Command needs raw data on enemy shield impacts. For the first 3 Rounds of the game, players are strictly forbidden from targeting Enemy Shields. You may only fire on arcs where the enemy shields are already reduced to 0, or you must wait until Round 4 to fire freely.  
* **Overclocked Reactors:**  
  * *The Rule:* Safety limiters are disabled. All Player Ships generate \+1 CT per round. However, ANY time a Player Ship takes Hull damage (even 1 point), they must draw a Critical Damage Card.

### **Doctrine: Total Control**

*The Hegemony demands absolute operational silence and perfect execution.*

* **Strict Radio Silence:**  
  * *The Rule:* Comms are monitored and encrypted. During Phase 2 (The Command Phase), players are completely forbidden from speaking or gesturing to each other while assigning Command Tokens. If a player speaks, their ship's Sensors officer gains 1 Stress.  
* **Rigid Firing Lines:**  
  * *The Rule:* Formations must be maintained. Player Ships may only target Enemy Ships that are located entirely within their Forward 180-degree arc (Fore, Fore-Port, Fore-Starboard). Firing backwards is considered a breakdown of the battle line and is forbidden.
