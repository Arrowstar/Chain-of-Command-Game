# **Content Database: Chaos & Consequences**

## **1\. Officer Fumble Deck**

*Drawn immediately when an Officer's Stress exceeds their Limit.*

### **General Fumbles**

* **"I Can't Think\!" (Freeze):** Action canceled. CT refunded. Station Locked for the rest of the round.  
* **"Shut Up and Let Me Work\!" (Insubordination):** Action succeeds. Fleet loses \-1 Fleet Favor (FF) due to open insubordination.  
* **"Nerve Collapse" (Burnout):** Action canceled. CT wasted. Officer's Tactical Skill Die drops one tier until Stress reaches 0\.  
* **"Bickering" (Contagion):** Action succeeds. Add \+1 Stress to every other Officer on your dashboard.

### **Station-Specific Fumbles**

*If a General Fumble is not drawn, the overworked officer causes a catastrophic error specific to their console.*

#### **Helm (Navigation & Evasion)**

* **Overcompensated Burn:** In a panic, the pilot slams the thrusters too hard. The intended movement is canceled. Instead, roll a D6 to determine a random hex-face (1=Fore, 2=Fore-Starboard, etc.). The ship immediately moves 1 Hex in that exact direction, maintaining its current facing. If this pushes the ship into an asteroid or another ship, resolve collision damage immediately.  
* **Inertial Dampener Failure:** The movement action succeeds, but the violent, uncalculated maneuver throws the crew across the deck. The ship's Base Evasion is reduced by 2 for the remainder of the round, and the Captain immediately loses 1 unspent Command Token from their pool.  
* **Navigational Lockout:** The nav-computer freezes due to rapid, contradictory inputs from a stressed officer. The ship's current action is canceled. Furthermore, the ship cannot "Rotate" or move for the remainder of this round AND the next round. The ship drifts predictably, granting enemies a \-1 TN to hit it.

#### **Tactical (Weapons)**

* **Panic Fire:** Trigger-happy response to overwhelming threat alerts. The intended target is ignored. The Tactical Officer immediately fires the loaded weapon system at the *closest* valid target in the firing arc—**including Allied ships**. If multiple targets are equidistant, the Adversary Player (or AI logic) chooses the worst possible target for the fleet.  
* **Tunnel Vision:** The attack goes through against the intended target. However, Tactical reroutes all processor power to targeting that single enemy. The ship's Point Defense Cannons (PDCs) are entirely disabled for the round, leaving the ship defenseless against incoming Torpedoes and Fighters.  
* **Misfire / Power Surge:** The weapon does not fire and the CT is wasted. Furthermore, the weapon system overloads and burns out; it takes 1 point of internal damage and cannot be fired in subsequent rounds until Engineering spends a "Damage Control" action to repair it.  
* **Ordnance Jam:** In a rush to load heavy weapons, the torpedo/missile auto-loaders jam. The intended action fails. Any currently primed ordnance is rendered inert, and Tactical cannot use the "Load Ordinance" action in the following round.

#### **Engineering (Damage Control & Shields)**

* **Catastrophic Reroute:** Engineering attempts to fix the problem but blindly pulls power from the wrong grid. The intended action (e.g., restoring shields or repairing hull) succeeds, but the Adversary Player chooses one fully charged Shield Sector and instantly reduces it to 0\.  
* **Toxic Coolant Vent:** The intended action fails and the CT is wasted. A ruptured line floods the lower decks with toxic coolant. The ship immediately takes 1 unblockable Hull damage, and the Engineering station is Locked for both this round and the next round while emergency scrubbers clear the air.  
* **False Diagnostics:** The stressed officer reads the wrong screen and mistakenly reports the ship is fine. The action is wasted. For the rest of the round, whenever this ship takes Hull damage, the Armor Die is NOT rolled (treat the Armor roll as an automatic 0).

#### **Sensors (Electronic Warfare & Intel)**

* **Ghost Contacts:** The Sensors officer feeds faulty, panicked telemetry to the fleet's targeting network. The intended action is canceled. Instead, the closest Enemy Ship gains a \+3 to their Evasion (TN) against ALL Allied attacks for the remainder of this round.  
* **Comms Blackout:** The officer accidentally severs the ship's encrypted connection to High Command. The action is canceled. For the remainder of this round, this ship's player is completely cut off: they cannot spend shared Fleet Favor (FF), cannot receive benefits from FF, and cannot be the target of Allied support actions.  
* **Targeting Feedback Loop:** The sensor array gets stuck in a feedback loop, broadcasting aggressive radar noise. The intended action fails. This ship instantly becomes the highest priority target for all AI enemies on the board, overriding standard proximity targeting rules.

## **2\. Critical Damage Deck (Player Ships)**

*Drawn when taking 3+ Hull damage in one volley, or falling below 50% Hull. Requires Engineering (2 CT \+ 4+ on D6) to repair.*

* **Main Thrusters Offline:** Ship cannot move forward. Helm "Ahead Standard" action is locked.  
* **Coolant Leak:** Every time Engineering is used, the Engineering officer takes \+1 additional Stress.  
* **Bridge Hit:** The Captain is injured. Maximum Command Tokens generated during Phase 1 is permanently reduced by 1\.  
* **Magazine Explosion:** Take an immediate, unpreventable 2 Hull Damage. Discard this card after resolving.  
* **Shield Generator Offline:** Shields no longer naturally regenerate 1 point during Phase 4\.  
* **Targeting Array Damaged:** Tactical Volley Pools lose their Skill Die.

## **3\. Critical Damage Deck (Enemy Ships)**

*Applied to Hegemony ships when they take heavy damage.*

* **Weapons Disabled:** Ship cannot fire during its next activation step. Discard after 1 round.  
* **Engine Fire:** Ship takes 1 unblockable Hull damage at the start of Phase 4\.  
* **Comms Severed:** Ship ignores the current AI Tactic Card.
