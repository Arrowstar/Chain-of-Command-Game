# **Content Database: Officer Fumble Deck**

## **Mechanics Overview**

When a player assigns a Command Token (CT) that pushes an Officer's Stress meter past their designated Limit, the player immediately pauses their planning phase, draws 1 card from the Fumble Deck, and resolves it.  
*Fumbles override the intended action.* The Command Token is usually considered spent unless the card specifically refunds it.

## **1\. General Fumbles (Applies to Any Station)**

* **Card: "I Can't Think\!" (Freeze)**  
  * **Flavor Text:** *"Too many screens... too many alarms\! Just give me a second\!"*  
  * **Effect:** The Officer freezes. The intended action is canceled. Return the Command Token to the Captain's pool (it can be reassigned to another officer). However, this Officer's station is **Locked** for the remainder of the round and cannot be used.  
* **Card: "Shut Up and Let Me Work\!" (Insubordination)**  
  * **Flavor Text:** *"With all due respect, Captain, I know my station better than you do\!"*  
  * **Effect:** The Officer rejects the Captain's micromanagement. The action is executed exactly as planned with no penalty to the roll, but the open insubordination over the comms causes the fleet to lose **\-1 Fleet Favor (FF)**.  
* **Card: "Nerve Collapse" (Burnout)**  
  * **Flavor Text:** *A sharp crack echoes across the bridge as the officer slams their fist through the console monitor.*  
  * **Effect:** The intended action is canceled and the CT is wasted. Furthermore, until this Officer's stress is reduced to 0, their Skill Die is permanently "stepped down" by one tier (e.g., a Veteran D6 becomes a D4).  
* **Card: "Bickering / Blame Game" (Contagion)**  
  * **Flavor Text:** *"This is your fault\! If you had rotated the ship, my shields wouldn't be failing\!"*  
  * **Effect:** The intended action succeeds. However, the panicked shouting stresses out the rest of the crew. Add \+1 Stress to **every other** Bridge Officer on your dashboard.

## **2\. Station-Specific Fumbles**

*If a General card is not drawn, these fumbles trigger specific mechanical disasters based on the console being operated.*

### **A. Helm (Navigation & Evasion)**

* **Card: "Overcompensated Burn"**  
  * **Effect:** The ship does not move as intended. Instead, roll a D6 to determine a random hex-face (1=Fore, 2=Fore-Starboard, etc.). The ship immediately moves 1 Hex in that exact direction, maintaining its current facing. If this pushes the ship into an asteroid or another ship, resolve collision damage immediately.  
* **Card: "Inertial Dampener Failure"**  
  * **Effect:** The movement action succeeds, but the violent maneuver throws the crew. The ship's Base Evasion is reduced by 2 for the remainder of the round, and the Captain loses 1 unspent Command Token from their pool as they are thrown from their chair.

### **B. Tactical (Weapons)**

* **Card: "Panic Fire"**  
  * **Effect:** The intended target is ignored. The Tactical Officer immediately fires the loaded weapon system at the *closest* valid target in the firing arc—**including Allied ships**. If multiple targets are equidistant, the Adversary Player (or AI logic) chooses the target.  
* **Card: "Misfire / Power Surge"**  
  * **Effect:** The weapon does not fire. The CT is wasted. Furthermore, the weapon system overloads; it takes 1 point of internal damage and cannot be fired next round unless Engineering spends an action to repair it.  
* **Card: "Tunnel Vision"**  
  * **Effect:** The attack goes through against the intended target. However, Tactical reroutes all processor power to targeting. The ship's Point Defense Cannons (PDCs) are disabled for the rest of the round, leaving the ship defenseless against incoming Torpedoes and Fighters.

### **C. Engineering (Damage Control & Shields)**

* **Card: "Catastrophic Reroute"**  
  * **Effect:** Engineering attempts to fix the problem but pulls power from the wrong grid. The intended action (e.g., restoring shields) succeeds, but the Adversary Player (or AI logic) chooses one fully charged Shield Sector and instantly reduces it to 0\.  
* **Card: "Coolant Vent"**  
  * **Effect:** The intended action fails and the CT is wasted. Toxic coolant floods the lower decks. The ship immediately takes 1 unblockable Hull damage, and the Engineering station is Locked for this round and the next round while it vents.  
* **Card: "False Diagnostics"**  
  * **Effect:** The officer mistakenly reports the ship is fine. The action is wasted. For the rest of the round, whenever this ship takes Hull damage, the Armor Die is NOT rolled (treat the Armor roll as a 0).

### **D. Sensors (EW & Targeting)**

* **Card: "Ghost Contacts"**  
  * **Effect:** The Sensors officer feeds faulty telemetry to the fleet. The intended action is canceled. Instead, the closest Enemy Capital Ship gains a \+3 to their Evasion (TN) against ALL Allied attacks for this round.  
* **Card: "Comms Blackout"**  
  * **Effect:** The officer accidentally severs the ship's connection to High Command. The action is canceled. For the remainder of this round, this ship's player cannot participate in spending or receiving benefits from the shared **Fleet Favor (FF)** pool, and cannot receive buffs from Allied ships.
