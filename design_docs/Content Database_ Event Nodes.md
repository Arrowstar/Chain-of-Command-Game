# **Content Database: Event Nodes (Distress Signals & Anomalies)**

## **1\. Resolving Event Nodes**

When the fleet pathfinder selects an **\[Event\]** node on the Sector Map, combat is bypassed. Instead, the Fleet Admiral (or a designated reader) reads the Event Prompt aloud to the War Council.

* **The Vote:** The players must democratically vote on which Option to select. In the event of a tie, the player acting as Fleet Admiral for this Sector breaks the tie.  
* **Hidden Outcomes:** Some options require a die roll (D6) to determine the result, meaning the fleet is taking a calculated gamble.  
* **Immediate Resolution:** The mechanical effects of the chosen option are applied to the fleet instantly.

## **2\. The Event Roster**

### **Event 01: The Derelict Transport**

**Narrative:** *Your sensors pick up a Hegemony civilian transport drifting near a shattered asteroid. Its life support is failing. They are hailing you on open comms, begging for emergency power and repairs.*

* **Option A: "Strip it for parts."** *The Hegemony would do the same to us.*  
  * **Effect:** Gain **\+30 RP**. Lose **\-2 FF**. Every Bridge Officer gains \+1 Stress.  
* **Option B: "Divert emergency power to their systems."** *We are not the monsters they say we are.*  
  * **Effect:** Lose **\-10 RP** (spent on parts/fuel). Gain **\+2 FF**. All Bridge Officers recover 1 Stress due to a morale boost.  
* **Option C: "Leave them to their fate."** *We can't afford the delay.*  
  * **Effect:** Nothing happens. Proceed to the next node.

### **Event 02: The Defector**

**Narrative:** *A single, heavily damaged Hegemony interceptor drops out of warp right in front of your fleet. The pilot immediately powers down their weapons and transmits a defection code, claiming to have high-level tactical data.*

* **Option A: "Bring them aboard. We need that intel."**  
  * **Effect:** Roll a D6.  
    * **1-2 (Saboteur\!):** The pilot detonates an EMP in your hangar. Lose **\-2 FF** and randomly select one player ship; that ship permanently loses 1 Internal Subsystem slot.  
    * **3-6 (Genuine Intel):** The data is invaluable. Immediately reveal the contents of the next 3 nodes on the Sector Map, and gain **\+15 RP** from their ship's scrap.  
* **Option B: "It's a trap. Space them."**  
  * **Effect:** The interceptor is obliterated. Lose **\-1 FF**. The Tactical Officer of the ship that fired the shot gains a permanent **Trauma Trait** from the guilt.

### **Event 03: The Nebula Storm**

**Narrative:** *The fastest route to the next sector jump-point goes directly through a violently unstable Ion Nebula. Rerouting around it will cost precious fuel and give the Hegemony time to close the gap.*

* **Option A: "Punch through. Brace for turbulence\!"**  
  * **Effect:** Every player ship must roll a D6. On a 1 or 2, that ship takes **2 unblockable Hull damage** from static discharges. On a 3-6, they pass through safely.  
* **Option B: "Reroute. It's too dangerous."**  
  * **Effect:** The fleet takes the long way. Lose **\-15 RP** (wasted fuel reserves), and every Helm officer in the fleet immediately gains \+2 Stress.

### **Event 04: Lower Decks Mutiny**

**Narrative:** *Rations are low, shifts are running 20 hours long, and the crew is cracking under the pressure of being hunted. A delegation from the engineering decks has barricaded themselves in the mess hall, demanding shore leave and double rations.*

* **Option A: "Crack down. We are at war."** *Send security teams to break the barricade.*  
  * **Effect:** Order is restored violently. Lose **\-3 FF** (the Admiral is displeased with your command). One randomly selected Engineering officer gains a permanent **Trauma Trait**.  
* **Option B: "Concede to their demands."** *Open the reserves. We need them functional.*  
  * **Effect:** Lose **\-25 RP** (spent on extra rations/supplies). Every Bridge Officer in the fleet immediately reduces their Stress to 0\.

### **Event 05: The Black Market Broker**

**Narrative:** *You pick up a scrambled transmission from a hollowed-out asteroid. It's a black market arms dealer willing to trade highly illegal, experimental Hegemony hardware—if you have the right currency.*

* **Option A: "Trade our reputation."**  
  * **Effect:** Spend **\-4 FF**. Gain one random **Experimental Tech** (Relic).  
* **Option B: "Trade our structural integrity."**  
  * **Effect:** The dealer needs rare alloys. Choose one Player Ship. That ship permanently reduces its Maximum Hull by \-3. Gain **\+40 RP**.  
* **Option C: "We don't deal with scum."**  
  * **Effect:** Leave the sector. Nothing happens.

### **Event 06: The Distress Beacon**

**Narrative:** *An automated Hegemony distress beacon is blaring across all frequencies from a nearby debris field. It could be a trap, or it could be a goldmine of salvage.*

* **Option A: "Investigate the debris."**  
  * **Effect:** Roll a D6.  
    * **1-3 (Ambush\!):** It's a trap. This node immediately transforms into a **\[Combat\]** node. Generate enemies normally, but the AI Threat Budget is increased by \+5 points.  
    * **4-6 (Jackpot):** You find an intact Hegemony supply cache. Gain **\+35 RP** and a free **Hull Patch** (restore 1 Hull) for every ship in the fleet.  
* **Option B: "Ignore it. Maintain radio silence."**  
  * **Effect:** Lose **\-1 FF** for ignoring standard galactic emergency protocols.

### **Event 07: The Zealous Inquisitor**

**Narrative:** *A Hegemony Inquisitor transmission hijacks your comms. They offer full pardons for the rest of the crew if the War Council simply surrenders their highest-ranking officer.*

* **Option A: "Never surrender."**  
  * **Effect:** The Inquisitor promises death. In your *next* **\[Combat\]** node, the Enemy Fleet automatically spawns with a Flagship (the heaviest ship gains \+1 Evasion and \+5 Hull).  
* **Option B: "Fire the Captain."**  
  * **Effect:** You fake a mutiny to appease them. The player acting as Fleet Admiral permanently loses 1 Maximum Command Token (CT Gen) for the rest of the run. Gain **\+3 FF**.

### **Event 08: The Viral Payload**

**Narrative:** *Your sensors isolate a dormant data packet echoing through a local comms buoy. It's a highly sophisticated Hegemony cyber-weapon left over from a previous skirmish. It might be weaponized, or it might infect your own grid.*

* **Option A: "Download and adapt it."**

    * **3-6 (Weaponized):** You isolate the code. Gain one random **Experimental Tech** (Relic).  
* **Option B: "Purge it from the buoy."**  
  * **Effect:** Lose **\-2 FF** for wasting time, but all Sensors officers reduce their Stress to 0\.

* **Option B: "Requisition their fuel."**  
  * **Effect:** You forcefully take their reserves. Gain **\+25 RP**. Lose **\-4 FF**. Every Bridge Officer gains \+2 Stress due to the ethical breach.

* **Option A: "Thread the needle."**

* **Option B: "Blast a path through."**  
  * **Effect:** You use your deflector arrays as a battering ram. Every player ship takes **1 unblockable Hull damage**, but the fleet gains **\+10 RP** from scavenging the detonated mine casings.

### **Event 11: The Mad Hermit**

**Narrative:** *You intercept a garbled, rambling transmission from a hollowed-out comet. An old, disgraced Hegemony engineer lives there, claiming to have perfected a revolutionary reactor design.*

* **Option A: "Buy the schematics."**  
  * **Effect:** Spend **\-30 RP**. Gain one random **Experimental Tech** (Relic).  
* **Option B: "Draft them into service."**  
  * **Effect:** The hermit joins your crew. Select one Engineering officer; upgrade their rank to the next tier for free (e.g., Veteran to Elite). Lose **\-2 FF** for bringing an unsanctioned civilian onto the bridge.

### **Event 12: Micro-Meteoroid Storm**

**Narrative:** *A silent, deadly wave of high-velocity micro-meteoroids is sweeping toward the fleet. There isn't time to jump away.*

* **Option A: "Overcharge the deflectors\!"**  
  * **Effect:** The shields hold, but the power drain is immense. At your *next* **\[Combat\]** node, all player ships start with exactly 0 Command Tokens during Round 1\.  
* **Option B: "Lock down and brace for impact."**  
  * **Effect:** Every player ship takes **2 unblockable Hull damage**, but you save your power reserves.

### **Event 13: The Propaganda Broadcast**

**Narrative:** *Hegemony psychological warfare divisions are blanketing all frequencies with demoralizing propaganda about your inevitable execution.*

* **Option A: "Jam the signal."**  
  * **Effect:** Spend **\-1 FF**. All Sensors officers take \+1 Stress.  
* **Option B: "Let them listen. We know the truth."**  
  * **Effect:** The psychological toll is heavy. Every Bridge Officer gains \+1 Stress.  
* **Option C: "Broadcast a defiant response."**  
  * **Effect:** Gain **\+2 FF** for fleet morale. However, in your *next* **\[Combat\]** node, the Enemy Threat Budget is increased by \+4 points because you gave away your position.

### **Event 14: Automated Defense Platform**

**Narrative:** *You drift into the sensor range of an ancient, automated orbital defense battery. It hasn't recognized your defector IFF codes yet, but its targeting lasers are warming up.*

* **Option A: "Hack the mainframe."**  
  * **Effect:** Roll a D6.  
    * **1-3 (Target Locked):** The hack fails. The battery fires. Every player ship takes **3 unblockable Hull damage**.  
    * **4-6 (Override Successful):** You rip its databanks clean. Gain **\+40 RP**.  
* **Option B: "Full reverse\! Get out of its range."**  
  * **Effect:** Every Helm officer takes \+2 Stress, but the fleet escapes unharmed.

### **Event 15: The Stowaway**

**Narrative:** *Security teams have found a Hegemony loyalist hiding in the cargo bay of one of your ships. They claim to have been left behind by mistake, but they could be a saboteur.*

* **Option A: "Interrogate them for fleet movements."**  
  * **Effect:** The interrogation is brutal. The Admiral is pleased (+2 FF). However, the Captain of the ship where the stowaway was found gains a permanent **Trauma Trait**.  
* **Option B: "Throw them out the airlock."**  
  * **Effect:** Brutal, but safe. Lose **\-2 FF**. Every Bridge Officer takes \+1 Stress.  
* **Option C: "Recruit them."**  
  * **Effect:** Roll a D6.  
    * **1-3 (Betrayal):** They sabotage your stores. Lose **\-20 RP**.  
    * **4-6 (Loyal):** They are a master mechanic. Immediately clear one **Ship Scar** from any ship in the fleet for free.

* **Option A: "Send engineering teams to stabilize it."**  
  * **Effect:** All Engineering officers gain \+3 Stress. You save the ship and its cargo: Gain **\+25 RP** and **\+1 FF**.  
* **Option B: "Tow it away and let it blow."**  
  * **Effect:** The explosion damages your towing cables. In your *next* **\[Combat\]** node, the Max Speed of all player ships is reduced by 1\.

  * **Effect:** Lose **\-3 FF**.

### **Event 19: The Smuggler's Cache**

### **Event 21: Blind Spot**

**Narrative:** *Due to a massive electromagnetic shadow from a nearby gas giant, your fleet practically bumps into a Hegemony patrol. Neither side has raised shields yet. You have seconds to act.*

* **Option A: "Ambush them\!"**  
  * **Effect:** This node transforms into a **\[Combat\]** node. However, during Round 1, all Hegemony ships have 0 Shields, and the player fleet acts first in all Initiative steps.  
* **Option B: "Quietly reverse course."**  
  * **Effect:** You slip away into the shadow. Lose **\-15 RP** (dumping cargo to reduce signature), but bypass the fight safely.  
* **Option C: "Ram them before they power up\!"**  
  * **Effect:** You smash through their formation. Every player ship takes **2 unblockable Hull damage**, but you completely destroy the patrol and gain **\+30 RP** from the wreckage, bypassing standard combat.

    * **1-3 (Logic Bomb):** The AI corrupts your systems. Randomly select one equipped Weapon Module in the fleet; it is permanently destroyed and removed.  
    * **4-6 (Tactical Genius):** The AI optimizes your grid. Gain one random **Experimental Tech** (Relic).  
* **Option B: "Wipe it and sell the hardware."**  
  * **Effect:** Gain **\+20 RP**.
