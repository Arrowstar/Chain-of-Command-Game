# **Campaign Guide: Sector Map Survival (Roguelite Mode)**

## **Overview: The Hunted Fleet**

*Chain of Command* is fundamentally about survival. In the Sector Map campaign, your task force has broken away from Hegemony High Command. You are outgunned, under-supplied, and actively being hunted by the main armada.  
To survive, you must navigate through a series of procedurally generated Sector Maps, jumping from node to node. Every battle drains your resources, every hit damages your hull, and every tense order traumatizes your crew. You must scavenge, upgrade, and fight your way to the final jump point before the Hegemony dreadnoughts catch up.

## **1\. Navigating the Sector Map**

The campaign takes place across three distinct **Sectors**, each culminating in a boss-tier engagement. A Sector Map is a branching web of interconnected nodes. At the start of a Sector, the fleet starts on the far left node and must chart a path to the Exit Node on the far right.  
The fleet moves as a single unit. Players must unanimously agree on which branching path to take. You can never move backward.

### **Node Types**

* **Hostile Patrol (Combat):** A standard tactical engagement. Players fight a generated Hegemony squad to achieve a basic objective (e.g., survive 5 rounds, destroy all enemies).  
* **Hazard Zone (Environmental):** A battle taking place in extreme conditions (e.g., an Ion Storm or Dense Asteroid Field). Evasion and navigation are prioritized over raw firepower.  
* **Distress Signal (Event):** A narrative text event. Players must vote on a course of action. *Example: You find a crippled civilian transport. Do you spend 10 Requisition Points to repair them for a chance at a unique crew member, or do you strip them for parts to gain 15 Requisition Points?*  
* **Hidden Cache (Loot):** A brief, highly dangerous combat scenario where players have only 3 rounds to snatch supply crates off the board before an overwhelming enemy force arrives.  
* **The Drydock / Black Market (Shop):** A safe haven where no combat occurs. Players spend their hard-earned Requisition Points (RP) to repair, upgrade, and re-arm.  
* **Sector Blockade (Boss):** The final node of a sector. Features a massive, unique Hegemony threat (e.g., a heavily fortified Starbase or a prototype Super-Dreadnought).

## **2\. The Campaign Economy**

The traditional "Fleet Favor" system adapts in the campaign. Because you are defectors, "High Command" is no longer the Hegemony—it represents the combined political will and shared resources of your scrappy rebel fleet.

### **Earning Requisition Points (RP)**

During a node, players earn **Fleet Favor (FF)** by completing the specific scenario Directives.  
At the end of the combat node, the fleet's total accumulated FF is converted into **Requisition Points (RP)**.

* **Conversion Rate:** 1 FF \= 10 RP.  
* **Salvage:** Players also gain bonus RP based on how many Hegemony capital ships they successfully destroyed during the node.

RP is a shared fleet resource. Players must democratically decide how to spend it at Drydocks. Will you pool your RP to buy the Aegis Carrier player a Fighter Hangar, or will you split it so everyone can repair their hulls?

## **3\. Persistent Damage & Trauma**

Unlike standalone skirmishes, nothing resets for free when a battle ends. You carry your burdens with you.

### **A. Persistent Hull & Criticals**

If your Paladin-Class Cruiser ends a battle with 4 Hull remaining and the "Coolant Leak" critical damage card active, you will start the *next* battle on the new node with exactly 4 Hull and the Coolant Leak still active.

* **Patching Up:** At a Drydock node, players can spend RP to repair Hull points or clear Critical Damage cards.

### **B. Ship Scars (Permanent Debuffs)**

When you repair a Critical Damage card at a Drydock, the card is removed, but it leaves a permanent **Scar** on that specific ship chassis. A repaired ship is never as good as it was fresh off the assembly line.

* *Example:* You pay to remove the "Main Thrusters Offline" critical card. The ship regains its ability to move, but it gains the Scar: *"Sluggish Thrusters: Maximum Speed is permanently reduced by 1."*  
* Scars cannot be removed. If a ship accumulates too many Scars, it might be time to abandon the chassis and buy a new one.

### **C. Officer PTSD & Trauma**

Stress is a killer. If a Bridge Officer maxes out their Stress during a mission and draws an **Officer Fumble Card**, the immediate battlefield effect resolves as normal. However, at the end of the mission, that officer gains a **Trauma Trait**.

* *Example Trauma (Gun-Shy):* "If this officer is assigned to Tactical, the Volley Pool loses 1 Weapon Die."  
* *Example Trauma (Paranoid):* "If this ship has 0 Shields in any sector, this officer passively gains 1 Stress every round."  
* **Treatment:** Trauma can be cured by spending RP at a Medical Station (found at Drydocks), or by rotating the traumatized officer off the active bridge crew and replacing them with a Rookie from the barracks.

## **4\. Upgrading the Fleet (The Drydock)**

When the fleet reaches a Drydock or Black Market node, players enter the Upgrade Phase.

### **The Starting Loadout**

At the beginning of Sector 1, players can only choose from the **Small** or **Medium** ship chassis (e.g., Vanguard, Zephyr, Manticore). They start with only basic Mark IV Plasma Batteries and no Internal Subsystems. Their bridge crew starts at the **Veteran (D6)** rank.

### **Spending RP**

At the Drydock, the fleet can spend their pooled RP on:

1. **Hull & Critical Repairs:** \* **Hull Repair:** Pay **5 RP** per Hull point restored.  
   * **Clear Critical Damage:** Pay **15 RP** per card to remove debilitating effects. *(Note: Repairing critical systems leaves a permanent Ship Scar on the chassis).*  
2. **Medical Treatment:** \* **Stress Relief:** Pay **2 RP** per Stress point removed from an officer.  
   * **Cure Officer Trauma:** Pay **20 RP** per Trauma Trait to fully rehabilitate an officer.  
3. **Weapon Modules:** Purchase heavy weapons like the Railgun or Torpedo Tubes. Once purchased, a module can be swapped between any ship in the fleet that has the appropriate slots. *(Refer to the Weapons & Subsystems Database for specific RP costs).*  
4. **Internal Subsystems:** Purchase powerful Active Upkeep nodes like the Auxiliary Reactor or ECM. *(Refer to the Weapons & Subsystems Database for specific RP costs).*  
5. **New Hulls (Chassis Upgrades):** Players can spend a massive amount of RP to "trade-in" their current ship and upgrade to a brand new chassis, such as unlocking the massive Minotaur-Class Dreadnought. *(Refer to the Ship Chassis Database for base RP costs).*  
6. **Officer Training:** Officers do not gain experience passively. To level up an officer and improve their Skill Die for the Volley Pool, the fleet must spend RP to run them through advanced combat simulations.  
   * **Rookie (D4) \-\> Veteran (D6):** 15 RP  
   * **Veteran (D6) \-\> Elite (D8):** 30 RP  
   * **Elite (D8) \-\> Legendary (D10):** 50 RP  
   * *Note:* An officer cannot be trained if they are currently suffering from a Trauma Trait. They must be cured via Medical Treatment first.

## **5\. Death & Reinforcements**

In a roguelite, death must be punishing but not game-ending for the player sitting at the table.

### **Mid-Battle Destruction**

If a player's Hull is reduced to 0 during a combat node, their ship is destroyed.

### **Post-Battle Reinforcements**

If the surviving players manage to win the node and jump away, the destroyed player rejoins the fleet. However, the loss of their specialized ship is a massive blow to the rebellion.

* **The Vanguard Respawn:** The destroyed player is issued a basic **Vanguard-Class Light Cruiser**.  
* **Loss of Gear:** Any Weapon Modules or Internal Subsystems that were equipped on the destroyed ship are lost to the void.  
* **Rookie Crew:** The player's previous bridge officers were KIA. They must staff their new Vanguard with "Rookie" officers (All Skill Dice are reduced to D4s). These replacement officers will remain Rookies until the fleet spends RP to train them in the Simulator.

If the *entire* fleet is destroyed during a combat node, the campaign is lost. High Command's armada catches up, and the rebellion is snuffed out. Time to start a new run\!
