# **Content Database: Campaign Progression (The Sector Map)**

## **1\. The Campaign Structure**

A standard campaign consists of surviving three consecutive **Sectors** (Acts).  
At the start of a Sector, players generate or reveal a branching "Web Map" consisting of 15 tiers of nodes, ending in a final Sector Blockade (Boss).

* **Movement:** The fleet moves as a single unit. Players must vote on which branching path to take. You can only move forward; you can never backtrack. In the event of a tie, the player acting as "Fleet Admiral" for that round breaks the tie.  
* **Persistence:** Hull damage, Critical Damage cards (which turn into permanent Scars), and Officer Stress (which turns into Trauma) persist between all nodes.

## **2\. Node Types**

As the fleet jumps through the sector, they will encounter different icons on the map.

* **\[Combat\] Hostile Patrol:** A standard procedural engagement.  
  * *Resolution:* Roll on the Procedural Scenario Generator (Objectives, Environment, AI Deployment).  
  * *Reward:* Standard Fleet Favor (FF) conversion to Requisition Points (RP).  
* **\[Elite\] Hunter-Killer Squadron:** A highly lethal combat encounter.  
  * *Resolution:* Generates like a normal combat, but the AI Threat Budget is increased by 50%, and the Hegemony fleet is guaranteed to spawn at least one ship one tier higher than the Sector usually allows.  
  * *Reward:* High FF/RP payout, PLUS a guaranteed **Experimental Tech** (a passive fleet-wide buff, functioning like an StS Relic).  
* **\[Event\] Distress Signal / Anomaly:** A text-based narrative encounter. No combat occurs.  
  * *Resolution:* The War Council is presented with a scenario and a set of choices with hidden or explicit consequences.  
  * *Example:* "You find a derelict Hegemony supply barge. Do you strip it for parts (+30 RP, but \-1 FF for delaying), or spend 1 FF to access its databanks and reveal the next 3 nodes on the map?"  
* **\[Haven\] Hidden Drydock:** The equivalent of a Campfire/Shop. No combat.  
  * *Resolution:* Players enter the Upgrade Phase. They spend their pooled RP to repair ships, heal the crew, and buy new weapons/subsystems.  
* **\[Boss\] Sector Blockade:** The 15th and final node of a Sector.  
  * *Resolution:* A fixed, massive encounter with a unique objective (e.g., destroying a Starbase, or surviving an ambush by a Hegemony Super-Carrier).  
  * *Reward:* Massive RP payout, full shield restore, and a guaranteed Epic Weapon drop before jumping to the next Sector.

## **3\. The Post-Combat Loop**

The moment a combat scenario ends (either via Victory or jumping to warp), the game shifts to the **At-Ease Phase**.  
**Step 1: The Payout**  
Convert all accumulated Fleet Favor (FF) from the mission (Objectives \+ Commendations) into **Requisition Points (RP)** at a rate of 1 FF \= 10 RP. Add this to the War Council's shared pool. FF then resets to 0\.  
**Step 2: Trauma Assessment**

* If an Officer ends the mission with their Stress meter entirely full, they immediately clear their Stress to 0 but gain a **Trauma Trait** (a permanent debuff, e.g., "Paranoid: Cannot use actions that cost 2+ CT").  
* All *other* officers reset their Stress to 0\. The jump through hyperspace allows them to sleep.

**Step 3: Damage Consolidation**

* Hull points do not regenerate.  
* If a ship has active **Critical Damage Cards** (e.g., "Weapons Offline"), the immediate crisis is stabilized, but it becomes a **Ship Scar**. Flip the card over. The ship now has a permanent minor debuff (e.g., "Scorched Relays: Primary weapon range is reduced by 1") until repaired at a Drydock.

## **4\. The Hidden Drydock (Upgrades & Healing)**

When the fleet reaches a Haven node, they may spend their pooled RP. This is where the deck-building/RPG mechanics shine.  
**The Services:**

* **Hull Patch (5 RP):** Restore 1 Hull point to one ship.  
* **Scrap Subsystem (+15 RP):** Sell an equipped weapon or subsystem you no longer want.  
* **Psych Eval (20 RP):** Cure one Officer's Trauma Trait.  
* **Deep Repair (30 RP):** Remove one Ship Scar.

**The Market:**  
The Drydock will have a randomly generated inventory of modules available for purchase.

* Draw 3 Weapon Cards and 3 Subsystem Cards from the armory deck.  
* Players may purchase these for their listed RP costs (e.g., Heavy Railgun for 40 RP) and instantly slot them into any compatible ship chassis.

**Officer Training:**  
You may spend RP to upgrade your crew's proficiency (increasing the die they add to the Volley Pool).

* Rookie (D4) to Veteran (D6): **15 RP**  
* Veteran (D6) to Elite (D8): **30 RP**

## **5\. Experimental Tech (Relics)**

Defeating Elites or completing difficult Events awards **Experimental Tech**. These are passive, rule-breaking artifacts shared by the entire fleet.

* **Inertial Dampeners:** The first time any player ship would take collision damage in a battle, negate it.  
* **Recycled Coolant:** Once per round, any Officer may ignore the \+1 Fatigue Penalty when repeating an action.  
* **The Admiral's Black-Box:** Start every combat node with \+1 Fleet Favor already on the track.  
* **Salvager Drones:** Earn \+5 RP for every Hegemony Small Craft (Corvette/Interceptor) destroyed.

## **6\. Game Over State**

* **Ship Destruction:** If a player's ship is destroyed, they lose their chassis, weapons, and crew. At the next node, they are issued a basic Vanguard Cruiser with starting weapons and Rookie (D4) officers.  
* **Total Wipe:** If the entire fleet is destroyed during a combat node, the run is over. The campaign resets to Sector 1, Node 1\.
